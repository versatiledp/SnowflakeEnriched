Preface¶

It was an autumn day of 2020 with COVID lockdown, our office team was working on data engineering research using mulitple data platforms like Azure Synapse, Databricks, and Snowflake. Having always believed in the power of education, Raj began considering writing a book on end-to-end data engineering aspects. He met with his colleague Babu over coffee in the evening and talked about it.

Raj and Babu decided to take upon the venture of writing a book on Snowflake for the SQL developer community, and eventually asked another colleague Siva to join along in the journey. Together, we made a commitment of three hours a day, three days a week, over the next three months of our time to make our dream come true. Looking back, our experience has taught us that one only needs the dedication to achieve success.

Throughout this experience, we have shared a common goal of assisting those deprived of education in India, and decided to contribute to the NGO Akshaya Patra. We believe there lies a significant potential hidden under the burdens of inequality. We are dedicating this book to all the children who suffer from malnutrition on a daily basis. We would greatly appreciate any donations made to https://www.akshayapatra.org/onlinedonations.

The main objective of the book is to lay-out a complete data platform solution that solves business stakeholder’s needs. We have included hands-on code throughout the book. The user community can take datasets from this book and execute the code. This will provide end-to-end coding exposure in the Snowflake data platform.

Many existing books have scattered examples to cover different aspects of the underlying technology. We instead wanted to employ a project-driven approach, consisting of data collection, modeling, ingestion, transformation, and visualization. In the current developer community using SQL for existing relational database solutions, this book will serve as a tool for software development using Snowflake.

We hope readers will find this book easy to follow and helpful for learning the Snowflake.

About the authors¶

Raj Rathi has been working in data management and data science, designing solutions from the ground up over the last 20+ years. His design thinking in data domain is completely based on the business-first approach. He was the founder of an analytical company that helped credit unions align business processes closely through data analytics. He has a dual-degree MBA and a Master’s in engineering, holds a CFA charter, and is always interested in trying out new technologies to help the business grow.

Babu Abraham is a Senior Data Engineer and Architect with hands-on experience in designing and refactoring large enterprise data warehouses and associated ETLs for 15+ years. He is an engineering graduate in Computer Science, who has demonstrated strength by undertaking some of the challenging data warehouse projects in various business domains like healthcare, manufacturing, wholesale, and insurance using best practices.

Siva Pentela has been working in data analytics and enterprise application development over the last 15 years. He is involved in the development of innovative solutions from design, development to the final deployment. He is passionate about data engineering, automation, and process simplification. He has a master’s degree in computer science.
